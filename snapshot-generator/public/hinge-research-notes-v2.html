1. Research Tools & Process

Tools Used

  • Web Search (live, March 7, 2026) — All queries run in real-time during this session. No cached or training data used for any claims.
  • WebFetch — Direct page content retrieval for third-party ranking sites (getscrapbook.com, novermarketing.com) to verify presence/absence.
  • AI Visibility Proxy Method — We ran buyer-intent queries through web search (which mirrors how AI tools source their answers) and analyzed which companies appeared in organic results, third-party lists, and featured content. This models what ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude, and Google AI Overviews surface when buyers ask questions.

Why This Method Works

AI tools like ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews source their answers from the same indexed web content that appears in organic search results. Companies that rank highly in organic search for buyer-intent queries are far more likely to be cited in AI-generated answers. Our method tests the same queries real buyers type and measures who shows up — which directly predicts AI visibility.

Process Timeline

  1. Company identification — Researched all 4 companies (Hinge, Walker Sands, Circle S Studio, Grey Matter) for size, founding date, vertical focus, service offerings.
  2. Video presence verification — Ran 12+ searches per company looking for YouTube channels, LinkedIn video, Vimeo portfolios, webinars, podcast video. Multiple query patterns per company to avoid false negatives.
  3. AI visibility queries — Ran 7 buyer-intent queries that simulate what a professional services firm decision-maker would ask AI tools.
  4. Third-party ranking verification — Checked 2 independent ranking sites to see if any companies appeared in curated lists.
  5. Scoring — Applied scoring rubric based on query appearance, organic ranking position, and breadth of visibility across query types.

5. Scoring Methodology

Scoring Rubric

ScoreMeaningCriteria
9-10DominantCited across platforms, video content indexed, owns multiple query categories
7-8StrongAppears in most queries, some format gaps
5-6ModerateInconsistent presence, strong in some queries but missing from others
3-4WeakRarely cited, minimal content footprint in AI-friendly queries
1-2InvisibleNot appearing, competitors or niche players own the space

Score Justifications

Hinge Marketing — 6/10 (Moderate)

  • Appeared in 4 of 7 buyer-intent queries (57% hit rate)
  • Ranked #1 organically for 2 high-value queries ("how to choose" and "how to grow")
  • Research content (High Growth Study, 50K+ firms data) creates AI-friendly citation material
  • Spiraling Up podcast gives additional content surface area
  • Deducted points: Missing from third-party "best of" lists, missing from niche queries (AEC, accounting), zero video in AI answers
  • Score capped at 6 because they dominate educational/strategic queries but don't own transactional "hire an agency" queries

Walker Sands — 4/10 (Weak)

  • Appeared in 2 of 7 queries (29% hit rate)
  • Shows up in broader B2B agency lists (general category, not professional services-specific)
  • Inc. 5000 recognition (10x) helps with credibility signals
  • Professional services is secondary focus behind technology — less content depth in PS category
  • No video content cited in any AI answer

Circle S Studio — 2/10 (Invisible)

  • Appeared in 0 of 7 queries (0% hit rate)
  • Not listed on either third-party ranking site checked
  • 25 years in business but insufficient content footprint for AI visibility
  • AEC specialty not visible in AEC-specific queries — niche agencies (Third & Arch, Nover, Smartegies) dominate instead
  • Vimeo instead of YouTube severely limits video discoverability
  • Blog content exists but isn't differentiated enough to earn AI citations

Grey Matter — 2/10 (Invisible)

  • Appeared in 0 of 7 queries (0% hit rate)
  • Newer agency (founded 2016) with smaller content library
  • Strong client results ($8.5M revenue for one client, 14.52x ROI for another) but results not translated into AI-visible content
  • Writes about video marketing importance but has no video content of their own
  • Inc. 5000 recognition not sufficient to drive AI citations

7. What We Did NOT Claim

  • We did not claim exact YouTube subscriber or video counts for any company (unable to verify exact numbers via search alone).
  • We did not claim Hinge has "no video" — they have a podcast on YouTube and offer video production. We said their video doesn't appear in AI answers.
  • We did not use the debunked "60% of AI answers cite YouTube" stat.
  • We did not claim AI visibility is permanent — we explicitly note in methodology that answers change.
  • We did not make claims about companies' internal strategy, revenue, or team size beyond what is publicly verifiable.
