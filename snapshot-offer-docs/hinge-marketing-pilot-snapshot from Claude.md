## Gap Analysis

### Gap 1: Zero YouTube Presence in a YouTube-First AI World

Hinge Marketing has no discoverable YouTube channel. This is remarkable for a firm that offers video marketing as a service (per their website). YouTube is now the #1 social platform cited in AI-generated answers (16% of citations), yet Hinge has zero video content available for AI to cite.

**Impact:** As AI models increasingly weight video and YouTube content in their recommendations, Hinge's dominance in text-based AI results is vulnerable. Any competitor that builds a YouTube presence around professional services marketing topics will have a structural advantage.

### Gap 2: The Entire Category Is Video-Weak

None of the 4 firms analyzed have meaningful video presence. This is an open field. The first mover will own AI video citations for "professional services marketing" queries.

**Impact:** This is actually good news for Hinge — they're not behind a competitor. But the window is closing. As AI citation of video content grows (up 25% since January 2026), the first firm to produce consistent YouTube content on these topics will cement their position.

### Gap 3: Hinge's Podcast Content Isn't Surfacing in AI

Hinge team members have appeared on multiple podcasts (Breaking BizDev, others), but this audio/video content doesn't appear in AI results. The content exists but isn't formatted or distributed in a way AI can discover and cite it.

**Impact:** Hinge is creating video content (podcast appearances) but not capturing the AI visibility value from it. Uploading podcast video to YouTube with proper metadata would immediately create citable video content.

### Methodology Issues Found

1. **Competitor selection matters enormously.** The "right" 3 competitors for Hinge aren't obvious. G2 confuses Hinge Marketing with Hinge (dating app). Need to confirm competitors before every snapshot.
2. **Single-model bias.** Claude may over-index on content it was trained on. Hinge's strong blog + research library likely inflates their Claude score. ChatGPT and Perplexity may show different results.
3. **YouTube search was polluted.** "Hinge" returns dating app results. Need to use "hingemarketing" or "Hinge Marketing professional services" for cleaner results.
4. **Video data is hard to get without visiting YouTube directly.** Web search alone doesn't reliably surface channel stats. Need to visit youtube.com/@channelname directly.
5. **Lite mode (2 models, 10 prompts) is viable at 60–90 min for cold outreach.** This pilot confirms the time estimate.

### Changes to Methodology

- [ ] Add "verify competitor names with prospect/Dane before starting" as Step 0
- [ ] Add YouTube direct-visit step (not just web search) for video data
- [ ] For categories with name collision (like "Hinge"), add company URL to prompts as disambiguation
- [ ] Track which AI model Dane was using (version matters — GPT-4o vs GPT-4 gives different results)
