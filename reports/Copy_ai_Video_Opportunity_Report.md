## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Copy.ai has 15,500 YouTube subscribers and 359 videos but has not uploaded in 6 months. Its three closest competitors — Jasper, Writer, and Anyword — are either equally stalled or posting to near-zero engagement. This is a wide-open category: no company in the AI GTM platform space is winning on video. The first mover that commits to a consistent, strategy-backed video program will own the conversation in AI search results, LinkedIn feeds, and YouTube discovery at a time when 70% of B2B buyers watch video before purchasing.

## COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

**Jasper** (jasper.ai) is Copy.ai's most direct competitor. An AI marketing platform with $88M in revenue, 100K+ customers, and 28.3K YouTube subscribers. Jasper has only 23 videos total but two of them — brand sizzle reels — pulled 224K and 138K views (almost certainly ad-boosted). Organic reach on recent uploads runs 54-630 views. Jasper has the strongest format variety: product demos, a CMO interview series ("The Spotlight"), Semrush co-branded webinars, customer case studies, and SEO/AEO/GEO content. They upload roughly monthly. 47,579 LinkedIn followers. No branded podcast. Their CEO, Timothy Young, has appeared at Web Summit but does not post video on LinkedIn regularly. Jasper has a dedicated customer stories page with 15 case studies — all text, no video testimonials. [1][2][3]

**Writer** (writer.com) is the most video-active competitor. An enterprise AI platform with 1.28K YouTube subscribers and 192 videos. Writer uploads 2-3 times per week — the highest cadence in the group. They run two branded series: "The AI Playbook" (short educational clips, 5-6 min) and "Humans of AI" (a full podcast with guests like former Meta executives and CEOs). Their CEO May Habib hosts the podcast and is a WEF Young Global Leader. Despite this effort, organic views on recent uploads range from 22 to 117. One brand video pulled 137K (ad-boosted). 30,174 LinkedIn followers. Writer also has interactive product demos on their website. No community presence found on Reddit/Slack/Discord. [4][5][6]

**Anyword** (anyword.com) is the weakest on video. 801 YouTube subscribers, 50 videos, and the most recent upload is 2 years old. The channel is effectively dormant. Their highest-performing video hit 12K views (a product feature announcement). Most content is 2-3 years old with under 400 views. 25,691 LinkedIn followers. No podcast. Active blog but no video embeds on the homepage. No community presence found. Anyword's video absence makes it irrelevant as a video benchmark but significant as evidence that this category broadly underinvests in video. [7][8]

## KEY FINDINGS

1. **No one in this category is winning on video.** The highest organic view count on a recent (last 3 months) video across all four companies is 654 views (Copy.ai's "How to Build a How-to Writing Agent," 7 months ago). Every company's recent uploads perform in the double-to-low-hundreds range. This is a category-wide gap.

2. **Copy.ai has the largest video library (359 videos) but has gone dark.** Six months without an upload means the channel is losing algorithmic momentum. Meanwhile, Writer uploads 2-3x/week and Jasper uploads monthly. Copy.ai's library is an asset — but only if reactivated.

3. **Writer is the only competitor with a branded podcast ("Humans of AI") featuring its CEO.** This gives Writer a thought leadership advantage in AI search citations and podcast discovery. Copy.ai and Jasper have no equivalent. Paul Yacoubian has appeared on third-party podcasts (This Week in Startups, McKinsey, Riding Unicorns) but Copy.ai does not own a show.

4. **Jasper is the only competitor actively creating AEO/GEO content on video.** Three of Jasper's most recent uploads specifically address SEO/AEO/GEO optimization — directly positioning for the AI search shift. No other company in this group is doing this.

5. **Zero companies in this group use video testimonials.** Jasper has 15 customer stories on their website — all text. Writer has interactive demos but no customer video. Copy.ai has no visible customer story section. Video testimonials are a white space across the entire competitive set.

## WHY THIS MATTERS NOW: THE AI SEARCH SHIFT

The rules of B2B discovery are changing. Fast.

**YouTube content now appears in 16% of large language model (LLM) answers** according to Adweek and Bluefish Labs research from January 2026. [9] That means when a prospect asks ChatGPT, Perplexity, or Google AI Overview "what is the best AI platform for marketing teams," YouTube videos with structured, expert content are being surfaced as answers.

**58.5% of Google searches are now zero-click** — the searcher gets their answer without visiting a website (SparkToro/Datos, July 2024). [10] If Copy.ai's answer to "how to build GTM AI workflows" is a blog post, it may never get clicked. If it is a well-structured YouTube video with clear chapters and transcripts, it gets cited by AI and consumed directly.

**70% of B2B buyers are through their buying journey before they ever talk to sales** (6sense, 2024). [11] What are they consuming during that 70%? Content. And increasingly, video content — **70% of B2B buyers watch video during their purchase process** (Google/Think with Google). [12]

**73% of videos cited by AI search engines are expert or third-party content, not brand advertising** (Zenith AI, November 2025). [13] This means product demos, thought leadership, and educational content get cited. Brand sizzle reels (like Jasper's 224K-view video) do not.

**AI-referred traffic converts at 1.3x to 5x+ higher rates** than traditional search traffic, according to multiple industry sources. [14] A prospect who arrives via an AI-surfaced video has higher intent than one clicking a Google ad.

The takeaway: Copy.ai's absence from video is not just a content gap. It is a growing discovery gap. Every month without consistent, expert video content is a month where competitors — or, more importantly, new entrants — can claim the AI search real estate that Copy.ai should own.

## WHAT STAYING THE COURSE LOOKS LIKE

If Copy.ai maintains its current video posture (no uploads in 6+ months, no podcast, no video testimonials), here is the likely trajectory:

- **YouTube algorithm decay.** Channels that stop uploading lose recommendation priority. Copy.ai's 15.5K subscribers become a dormant asset rather than a distribution advantage.
- **Writer takes the thought leadership lane.** With 2-3 uploads per week and a CEO-hosted podcast, Writer is building the AI search corpus that LLMs will cite when answering enterprise AI questions. The gap widens every week.
- **Jasper owns the AEO/GEO conversation.** Jasper is already publishing videos specifically about optimizing for AI search. If a prospect asks an LLM "how do I optimize content for AI search engines," Jasper's videos are the ones that will surface.
- **No video testimonials means no social proof in the formats buyers prefer.** 70% of B2B buyers watch video. If Copy.ai's customer stories are text-only (or nonexistent), they lose the most persuasive format at the bottom of the funnel.
- **Nathan's product marketing content has no video distribution layer.** Product launches, feature announcements, and competitive positioning all perform better with video. Without it, Copy.ai relies entirely on text-based channels where attention is shrinking.
