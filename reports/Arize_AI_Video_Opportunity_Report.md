# Video Opportunity Assessment: Arize AI
### Prepared for Tammy Le, Chief Marketing Officer
### Prepared by Digital Accomplice | February 28, 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Arize AI has built a substantial YouTube library (595 videos, 8K subscribers) but is being massively outpaced by every direct competitor in video reach, format diversity, and audience growth. Weights & Biases has 8x the subscribers and a branded podcast driving six-figure view counts. LangChain has 21x the subscribers with fewer videos. The gap is not in effort -- Arize is publishing frequently -- it is in strategy, format, and distribution. The opportunity is to convert Arize's existing content engine into one that actually builds audience and drives pipeline.

---

## COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

**Weights & Biases (wandb.ai)** is the clear video leader in this space. Acquired by CoreWeave in 2025 for $1.7B, W&B has 63.9K YouTube subscribers across 967 videos, a branded podcast ("Gradient Dissent" hosted by CEO Lukas Biewald) that regularly features top-tier guests (Jensen Huang, GitHub CEO Thomas Dohmke, Cursor co-founder Sualeh Asif), and a multi-format content strategy that includes courses, shorts, live streams, and community posts. Their top video has 259K views. Their CEO is personally active on LinkedIn with video content. W&B treats video as a core growth channel, not a checkbox.

**Datadog (datadoghq.com)** brings enterprise-scale brand weight with 29.7K YouTube subscribers and 989 videos. Their top video hit 965K views. However, their recent upload engagement is weak -- recent videos pull only 150-250 views despite the subscriber base. Datadog also has 298K LinkedIn followers (30x Arize's count). Their content skews heavily toward product tutorials and conference recordings. They have a public Slack community and an ambassador program. Their ML/AI observability offering is newer, bolted onto their core infrastructure monitoring platform.

**LangChain (langchain.com)** has emerged as the dark horse video powerhouse in the AI tooling space with 171K YouTube subscribers on just 439 videos -- the highest subscriber-to-video ratio of any company in this audit. Their popular videos regularly crack 200K+ views (top: 258K on "How Uber Built AI Agents"). They run the "Interrupt" conference, have 50K Discord members, 15K Reddit community members, a Courses tab on YouTube, and 146K LinkedIn followers. LangChain's product (LangSmith) competes directly with Arize AX on LLM tracing and evaluation.

## KEY FINDINGS

1. **Arize has the lowest subscriber count of all four companies audited (8.08K) despite having more videos than LangChain (595 vs. 439).** LangChain gets 21x the subscribers with 26% fewer videos. This points to a format and discoverability problem, not an effort problem.

2. **Arize's recent videos average 100-300 views.** LangChain's recent videos average 2,500-10,000 views. Weights & Biases recent uploads range from 114 to 11K views. Arize is producing volume without audience traction.

3. **No competitor in this space has a strong website video strategy.** Arize's homepage is 100% static screenshots. This is a first-mover opportunity -- embedding product demo video, customer testimonial video, and explainer content on the homepage and product pages would differentiate Arize from every competitor audited.

4. **Arize has no branded podcast. Weights & Biases has "Gradient Dissent" -- one of the top AI podcasts -- hosted by the CEO.** Gradient Dissent episodes regularly feature household-name AI leaders and drive thousands of views per episode on YouTube. This is the single biggest content format gap for Arize.

5. **Arize's content is almost exclusively technical tutorials and conference recordings.** The channel lacks short-form explainers, customer stories, thought leadership from executives, and podcast-style conversational content. Weights & Biases and LangChain both use multiple video formats to reach different audience segments at different stages of the funnel.

## WHY THIS MATTERS NOW: THE AI SEARCH SHIFT

The way Arize's buyers find solutions is changing faster than most marketing teams realize.

**58.5% of Google searches now end without a click** [1]. Your prospects are getting answers -- including vendor recommendations -- directly from AI-generated summaries. If Arize's content is not structured to be cited by AI systems, it is invisible to a growing share of the market.

**YouTube content appears in 16% of LLM-generated answers** [2]. This is not a vanity metric. When an ML engineer asks an AI assistant "what tools should I use for LLM observability," YouTube videos from Weights & Biases and LangChain are being surfaced. Arize's 100-300 view videos are not.

**73% of videos cited by AI search engines are expert or third-party content, not ads** [3]. The AI search layer rewards depth, specificity, and authority. Tutorials, technical walkthroughs, and expert interviews -- exactly the kind of content Arize should be producing at higher quality and with better SEO.

**70% of B2B buyers are through their purchase journey before they ever talk to sales** [4]. In the AI observability space, this means ML engineers are evaluating Arize against Weights & Biases, LangChain, and Datadog based on the content they find online. Right now, Arize is outgunned in every channel.

**70% of B2B buyers watch video during the purchase process** [5]. And AI-referred traffic converts at 1.3x-5x+ higher rates than traditional search [6]. The compound effect: video content that gets cited by AI systems does not just build awareness -- it drives pipeline.

Arize's competitors are not waiting. Weights & Biases has built a content moat with Gradient Dissent. LangChain's subscriber growth (171K) suggests they are capturing the attention of the exact ML engineers Arize wants to reach. Every month without a strategic video response is a month those competitors extend their lead.

## WHAT STAYING THE COURSE LOOKS LIKE

If Arize continues publishing 2-3 technical tutorials per week at 100-300 views each:

- **12 months from now:** YouTube channel reaches maybe 10K subscribers (vs. W&B at 65K+ and growing, LangChain at 171K+). Arize remains invisible in AI search citations. No branded podcast means no access to the thought-leadership-driven top-of-funnel that W&B dominates.
- **The homepage stays static.** Every prospect who visits arize.com sees screenshots while W&B and Datadog show video demos. Conversion rates stay flat.
- **LinkedIn remains text-heavy.** Without video clips, podcast excerpts, or exec-led video content, Arize's ~9.8K followers see the same format as every other B2B tech company. No differentiation.
- **The "Observe" conference recordings continue to live and die on YouTube** without being repurposed into short-form clips, LinkedIn content, blog embeds, or AI-searchable assets.
- **LangChain's community lead widens.** 50K Discord members vs. Arize's 7.4K Slack members. Community drives content, content drives search visibility, search visibility drives pipeline. This flywheel is already turning for competitors.

The risk is not dramatic. It is gradual. Arize slowly becomes the company that ML engineers have "heard of" but do not think of first. In a market where 70% of the buying decision happens before a sales conversation, that is a pipeline problem.

## WHAT WE'D RECOMMEND -- 90-DAY STARTING POINT

**Month 1: Foundation**
- Audit and retitle the 20 highest-potential existing YouTube videos for search optimization (many current titles are conference-style, not search-friendly)
- Produce 1 high-quality product demo video (under 4 minutes) for the arize.com homepage
- Record 2 customer testimonial videos with existing champions (Tripadvisor, Siemens, or DoorDash would be ideal given their logo placement on the homepage)
- Launch a short-form video series: "60-Second Observability" -- one concept, one minute, designed for LinkedIn and YouTube Shorts
- Begin planning a branded podcast (working title: something tied to "AI in Production" or "Shipping Agents")

**Month 2: Podcast + Executive Content**
- Launch the podcast with Aparna Dhinakaran or Jason Lopatecki as host. First 3 guests should be recognizable names from Arize's customer base or advisory network
- Repurpose each podcast episode into: 1 full YouTube video, 3-5 LinkedIn clips (60-90 seconds), 1 blog post with embedded video, 1 audiogram for social
- Begin exec-led LinkedIn video: Aparna or Jason posting 1 short video per week (market commentary, product insight, or reaction to industry news)
- Publish 2 "How [Customer] Uses Arize" case study videos

**Month 3: Scale + Measure**
- Establish weekly publishing cadence: 1 podcast episode, 2 short-form videos, 1 tutorial/demo
- Embed video across all key website pages (homepage, product pages, pricing page)
- Track: YouTube subscriber growth rate, average view count on new uploads, LinkedIn video engagement vs. text post engagement, website page-with-video conversion rate vs. page-without-video
- Evaluate which content is being cited in AI search results (test with ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini queries about AI observability tools)

## WHAT THIS COULD LOOK LIKE

**1. "Shipping Agents" Podcast (Bi-weekly, 30-45 min)**
A conversation series hosted by Arize's co-founder where ML leaders from companies like Uber, DoorDash, Instacart, and Reddit talk about what it actually takes to get AI agents into production. Not product pitches -- real engineering war stories. This is the Gradient Dissent counter-move, but focused specifically on production AI and agent reliability, which is Arize's core positioning. Each episode becomes 5+ pieces of derivative content across YouTube, LinkedIn, blog, and social.

**2. "Observability in 60 Seconds" Short-Form Series (Weekly)**
One AI/ML concept explained in under 60 seconds. Topics: "What is model drift?", "LLM tracing explained", "Why your RAG pipeline is hallucinating", "Embeddings monitoring in 60 seconds." These are designed for LinkedIn native video, YouTube Shorts, and -- critically -- to be the kind of concise, authoritative content that AI search engines cite. This is the format LangChain uses to drive their outsized subscriber-to-video ratio.

**3. "Inside [Company]'s AI Stack" Customer Showcase (Monthly, 5-8 min)**
A produced video series that goes inside Arize customers' AI operations. Show the actual dashboards, the actual traces, the actual evaluation workflows. Feature the ML engineer or data scientist, not the executive. This format does not exist anywhere in the competitive set -- no one in AI observability is doing produced customer showcase video. Closest comparison: Datadog's case study pages, which are text-only. First-mover advantage is real here.
