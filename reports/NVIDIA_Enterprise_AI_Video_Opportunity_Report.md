# Video Opportunity Assessment: NVIDIA Enterprise AI

**Prepared for:** Shannon McPhee, Director of Communications, Enterprise AI Products & Partners, NVIDIA
**Prepared by:** Digital Accomplice
**Date:** February 28, 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

NVIDIA dominates enterprise AI hardware and software, but its video strategy is overwhelmingly event-driven — built around Jensen Huang keynotes and GTC livestreams — with almost no consistent, mid-funnel enterprise AI video content between tentpole moments. Google Cloud publishes daily educational video content to 1.3M YouTube subscribers with dedicated AI tutorials, while NVIDIA's 2.14M-subscriber channel averages just 2 videos per week, most of which skew toward gaming/GeForce rather than enterprise AI buyers. The gap is not in brand awareness — it is in the sustained, searchable, AI-citable video content that reaches the 70% of B2B buyers who decide before ever talking to sales [1].

---

## COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE

### AMD (Competitor 1 — Direct Hardware Rival)
AMD is NVIDIA's closest enterprise AI hardware competitor. The Instinct MI300X offers 192GB HBM3e memory and has been deployed by Microsoft, Oracle, and Meta. AMD's data center revenue hit $4.3B in Q3 2025, up 22% YoY [2]. At CES 2026, CEO Lisa Su unveiled the MI440X for on-premises enterprise AI and previewed the Helios rack-scale system delivering 3 AI exaflops per rack [3]. AMD recently signed a multi-year deal with Meta spanning multiple generations of Instinct GPUs [4]. Despite real product momentum, AMD's video marketing is minimal — their YouTube channel has just 320K subscribers and 787 videos, and enterprise AI content is buried under gaming and consumer content [5].

**Why selected:** Closest direct competitor in enterprise AI accelerators. Same buyer, same data center, same purchasing decision.

### Intel (Competitor 2 — Legacy Infrastructure + AI Challenger)
Intel is repositioning around enterprise AI with Gaudi 3 accelerators, claiming 50% better inference and 40% better power efficiency than NVIDIA H100 at lower cost [6]. Intel partnered with SambaNova in February 2026 for AI inference solutions and launched Crescent Island data center GPU targeting energy-efficient inference for 2026 [7]. Intel's YouTube has 567K subscribers and 1,212 videos, with roughly 1.25 videos per week [8]. Their "Intel on AI" podcast is active with enterprise-focused episodes. But Intel's video presence is scattered across too many product lines and lacks a cohesive enterprise AI narrative.

**Why selected:** Major incumbent in enterprise data centers. Every NVIDIA Enterprise AI buyer already has Intel infrastructure. Intel is actively trying to insert Gaudi into those environments.

### Google Cloud (Competitor 3 — Cloud AI Platform)
Google Cloud sells TPUs, Vertex AI, and Gemini-powered enterprise AI services. Their 7th-generation TPU Ironwood (November 2025) delivers performance analysts call "on par with Blackwell" [9]. Vertex AI usage grew 20x in the past year [10]. Google Cloud Tech's YouTube channel is the gold standard for enterprise tech video: 1.3M subscribers, 5,000+ videos, daily uploads, structured playlists, and dedicated AI tutorials [11]. Their video strategy is the benchmark NVIDIA's Enterprise AI division should measure against.

**Why selected:** Dominant cloud AI platform. Sells directly to the same enterprise buyers. Has the strongest video content operation of any company in this space.

## KEY FINDINGS

1. **Google Cloud publishes 7x more frequently than NVIDIA on YouTube** — daily uploads vs. ~2/week. On enterprise AI topics specifically, the gap is likely 10x or more, since most NVIDIA uploads are gaming/GeForce content [11][12].

2. **NVIDIA's video strategy is event-dependent.** Jensen Huang keynotes generate millions of views (22M for GTC 2023, ~700K for GTC 2025) [13][14]. But between events, enterprise AI video output drops to near zero. There is no "always-on" content engine.

3. **No company in this space is producing mid-funnel enterprise AI video consistently** — customer testimonials, use-case explainers, ROI stories, technical decision-maker content. Google Cloud comes closest with customer story videos, but even they lean heavily toward tutorials and developer content. This is a first-mover opportunity.

4. **NVIDIA has a branded podcast ("The AI Podcast") with 3.4M+ listeners and 285+ episodes** [22] — but it is audio-only with no video clips, no YouTube Shorts, and no LinkedIn video repurposing. This is an untapped content goldmine.

5. **YouTube now appears in 16% of all LLM answers** — more than Reddit [23]. Enterprise AI buyers increasingly discover vendors through AI-powered search. NVIDIA's keynote-only video strategy means their enterprise AI content is sparse in the very medium AI models are now citing most.

## WHY THIS MATTERS NOW: THE AI SEARCH SHIFT

The way enterprise buyers find vendors has fundamentally changed.

**58.5% of Google searches now end without a click** [24]. Buyers get their answer from the search results page itself — or from AI-generated summaries. If your content is not structured for AI citation, you do not exist in that answer.

**YouTube appears in 16% of all LLM-generated answers** — overtaking Reddit as the #1 social source cited by AI models [23]. The models read transcripts, metadata, and surrounding text. Long-form, keyword-rich video content is exactly what gets cited.

**70% of B2B buyers are through their purchasing process before they ever talk to sales** [1]. That means NVIDIA's enterprise AI prospects — CIOs, CTOs, ML engineers evaluating DGX vs. Google TPU vs. AMD MI300X — are forming opinions based on what they can find. Right now, they find Google Cloud tutorials. They find Jensen Huang keynotes. They do not find a steady stream of NVIDIA Enterprise AI content answering their specific questions.

**73% of AI-cited videos are expert or third-party content** [25] — not brand marketing. This means the winning format is not a polished product ad. It is an expert explaining a real problem and how they solved it with specific technology. NVIDIA's customers and partners are doing this work every day. None of it is being captured on video.

**AI-referred traffic converts 1.3x to 5x+ higher than organic search traffic** [26]. These are not casual browsers. They are buyers with intent, sent by an AI that determined NVIDIA's content was the best answer. But only if that content exists.

**70% of B2B buyers watch video during their purchase journey** [27]. Not keynotes. Not product ads. Explainers. Comparisons. Customer stories. Technical deep-dives. The content that answers the question: "Should we bet our AI infrastructure on NVIDIA?"

## WHAT STAYING THE COURSE LOOKS LIKE

NVIDIA's current enterprise AI video strategy relies on 2-3 tentpole events per year (GTC, CES, Computex) to carry the entire video narrative. Between events, the YouTube channel serves primarily gaming/GeForce audiences.

If this continues:

- **Google Cloud will own the "how-to" layer** of enterprise AI video. Every tutorial, every demo, every customer story they publish becomes a permanent, searchable, AI-citable asset. NVIDIA's enterprise AI story gets told by other people's content.

- **AMD is ramping.** The Meta multi-year deal, the MI440X launch, Lisa Su's CES keynote — AMD is building enterprise AI credibility fast. If AMD starts producing consistent enterprise AI video content before NVIDIA does, they will own the comparison narrative.

- **The AI citation window is closing.** AI models are being trained on current content. The companies that produce searchable, structured, expert-driven video content now will be the ones cited in AI answers for years. Absence from this content layer is not neutral — it is actively ceding ground.

- **NVIDIA's podcast (3.4M listeners, 285+ episodes) remains audio-only.** Every episode is a missed video asset. Every guest interview is a missed YouTube clip. Every expert insight is invisible to AI models that favor video transcripts.

## WHAT WE'D RECOMMEND — 90-DAY STARTING POINT

### Phase 1: Days 1-30 — Capture What Already Exists

NVIDIA's Enterprise AI division already has the raw material. The gap is not content creation from scratch — it is content capture and repurposing.

1. **Video-record 4 AI Podcast episodes.** The podcast already has guests, topics, and a production cadence. Adding a camera turns each episode into 1 full-length YouTube video + 5-8 short clips for LinkedIn and YouTube Shorts. Estimated output: 4 full episodes + 20-32 clips in Month 1.

2. **Produce 2 "Enterprise AI Explained" videos.** Short (3-5 min), expert-led videos answering real buyer questions: "What's the difference between DGX and HGX?" or "When does on-premises AI make more sense than cloud?" These are the videos that get cited by AI search.

3. **Capture 2 customer story videos.** NVIDIA's enterprise AI customers include Deutsche Telekom, Caterpillar, and SAP. A 90-second video of a CTO explaining why they chose DGX is more persuasive than any product page.

4. **Audit and retag existing enterprise AI video content on YouTube.** NVIDIA likely has enterprise-relevant content buried in GTC session recordings. Retitling, re-thumbnailing, and adding structured descriptions can surface this content in AI search results without producing anything new.

### Phase 2: Days 31-60 — Build the System

Establish a repeatable content engine: weekly video-podcast episode, biweekly explainer, monthly customer story. Define the editorial calendar. Set up the production workflow. Align with NVIDIA's enterprise AI product launch calendar. (Details in full engagement.)

### Phase 3: Days 61-90 — Connect to Pipeline

Measure what is working. Track AI citations. Monitor search visibility for enterprise AI buyer queries. Build the connection between video content and enterprise AI pipeline metrics. (Details in full engagement.)

## WHAT THIS COULD LOOK LIKE

### Content Idea 1: "The AI Infrastructure Decision" — Video Series (6 episodes)

A CTO-level series where real enterprise AI buyers walk through how they evaluated AI infrastructure options. Episode 1: "Why We Moved from Cloud TPUs to On-Premises DGX." Episode 2: "What We Wish We Knew Before Building Our First AI Factory." Each episode is 8-12 minutes, shot in real data centers, featuring real decision-makers. This is the content B2B buyers search for. It does not exist anywhere in this market right now.

### Content Idea 2: "AI Podcast — Now on Video" — Repurposed Series

Take the existing AI Podcast (285+ episodes, 3.4M listeners) and add video. Film the host and guest. Clip the best 60-90 second moments for LinkedIn and YouTube Shorts. Each episode produces 1 long-form YouTube video + 6-8 short-form clips. At biweekly cadence, that is 2 full videos and 12-16 clips per month — with zero new editorial planning required.

### Content Idea 3: "NVIDIA Enterprise AI in 90 Seconds" — Short-Form Explainer Series

Weekly 60-90 second videos answering one specific enterprise AI question. "What is NIM?" "How does CUDA-X accelerate inference?" "What's new in AI Enterprise 6.0?" Optimized for YouTube Shorts, LinkedIn, and AI search citation. One expert. One question. One clear answer. These are the building blocks of AI search visibility.
