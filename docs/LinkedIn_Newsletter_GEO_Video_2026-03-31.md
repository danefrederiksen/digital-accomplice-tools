# The GEO Strategy Most Marketers Are Missing (It's Video)

Generative Engine Optimization is the new SEO. You already know this.

ChatGPT. Perplexity. Google AI Overviews. Your buyers are getting answers from AI before they ever click a link. If your brand isn't in those answers, you're invisible at the most important moment in the buying process.

Most marketing teams I talk to understand this. They're already thinking about GEO — optimizing content for AI citation, building authority, going deeper on topic clusters.

What they're not doing is connecting video to any of it.

That's the gap. And it's a significant one.

---

## Why Video Feeds the AI Machine

Here's what most content strategists don't realize: AI models don't just pull from blog posts and landing pages.

They pull from transcripts. Structured text. Question-and-answer formats. Authoritative sources with clear topic signals.

Video — specifically YouTube video — checks every one of those boxes.

YouTube accounts for 31.8% of social AI citations. That's from OtterlyAI's analysis of over 100 million AI citation instances in March 2026. YouTube shows up in 16% of AI-generated answers. Reddit shows up in 10%. Your blog? Often far less.

YouTube AI Overview citations are up 25% year over year (BrightEdge).

Here's the stat that should stop you cold:

**40.83% of the YouTube videos being cited by AI had fewer than 1,000 views.**

Let that land. AI doesn't care if your video went viral. It cares whether the content is structured, authoritative, and relevant to the query.

That is a winnable game. Even for companies who aren't YouTube giants.

---

## But Video Is More Than a Technical Play

The GEO case alone would be enough to prioritize video. But there's a second reason — and it's just as important.

AI-generated content is everywhere now. 93% of marketers are using AI to produce content. Most of it reads the same. Sounds the same. Says roughly the same thing.

Your buyers feel it. 66% of them say AI content doesn't hold the same value as human stories (Clutch/Conductor, Feb 2026).

Video is the one format AI can't convincingly replicate.

Your founders. Your subject matter experts. Your clients talking about their outcomes. Those are human signals that build trust in a way a blog post — no matter how well-written — can't match.

So video does two things at once:

1. **Technical GEO/SEO lift.** Transcripts feed AI models. YouTube authority is indexed. Structured, topical content earns citation.
2. **Brand trust.** Real people, real stories, real expertise. This is your differentiation in a content landscape that's getting more synthetic every month.

You don't get that from any other single content format.

---

## The Part Everyone Gets Wrong

Okay. So you make some videos. Problem solved, right?

No.

Random videos don't build GEO authority. A product demo here, a conference recap there, an "excited to announce" clip — none of that creates the structured, topic-dense content that AI models cite.

What works is a program.

Not a campaign. A program.

That means:
- **Topic clusters, not one-offs.** Cover a subject from multiple angles, depth over breadth, over time. AI models reward authority built across a body of work.
- **Integrated with your SEO and GEO strategy.** Your video topics should map to the questions your buyers are asking AI. Not guesswork — keyword and query research.
- **Built around your business goals.** Lead gen? Retention? Partnership development? The video program structure changes depending on what you're trying to move.
- **Transcript-first thinking.** Every video you make is also a text asset. Optimize accordingly.

One more thing on this: video has repurposing efficiency no other format matches.

One 20-minute interview becomes: a YouTube video, a transcript (indexable content), a short-form clip series, a newsletter, pull quotes for LinkedIn, a podcast episode. You make the asset once. You distribute it everywhere AI crawls.

That multiplier matters when your team's time is limited.

## Where Most B2B Companies Are Right Now

You're probably doing some content. Maybe a blog, email newsletter, LinkedIn updates. Maybe you've experimented with video but it never got traction.

The mistake I see most often: you treated video like a production problem instead of a strategy problem.

You didn't get poor results because the camera quality was bad or the editing wasn't slick enough.

You got poor results because the video wasn't connected to a keyword strategy, didn't cover topics your buyers are searching for, and wasn't part of a consistent body of work that builds topical authority.

Production value matters less than structure. Consistency. Strategic intent.

The companies that figure this out in the next 12 months will be nearly impossible to displace in AI search for their category. The ones that don't will be playing catch-up against brands that built the library while they waited.
