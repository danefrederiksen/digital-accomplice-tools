## 3. Psychographics (Beliefs, Values, Fears)

> **Core tension (from 20+ prospect transcripts):** "The most talented, smart experts are also the busiest — their time is very precious, often very billable." SMEs are the most credible voices a company can put forward. They're also the least available to do it. DA resolves that.

### What They Believe
- Video is important but they haven't cracked it yet
- Content marketing still works, but the old playbook (blog + SEO + gated PDF) is dying
- AI is changing everything and they need to adapt, not ignore it
- Credibility and trust matter more than volume in B2B — "surface level content will hurt your brand"
- They need a partner, not just a vendor — "partnership between SME and content marketer"
- They want to become a "visible expert" / "visible firm" (Hinge language — widely recognized)

### What They Value
- Strategic thinking before execution — but note: the word "strategy" can scare some ("expensive + slow"). Use "structured approach" or "big picture plan" instead.
- Efficiency — "make it easier for them to contribute" / "content flywheel" / "feeds everything else"
- Low-friction entry — "baby steps" / "pilot project" / "test first" / "prove and show your value"
- Personal brand building for their SMEs — "what's in it for them"
- Sustainability — they don't want a one-off video, they want a "content machine"
- Data and measurement — they need to justify spend, tired of "vanity metrics" and "vague conversation of 'how's it going'"

### What They Fear
- Being invisible — "not the most visible expert in my niche" / "hidden behind white papers and ghostwriting"
- AI content flood making them disappear — "AI slop" / "inundated with bad content" / "competing with noise"
- Wasting their SMEs' time — "takes too much of their time — it all falls apart"
- Camera/visibility discomfort — "uncomfortable on video" / "get over that uncomfortable hurdle"
- No measurement — "can't tie content to business goals and objectives" / "no KPIs / don't know what good looks like"

### What Frustrates Them
- SMEs are buried in delivery — "deep in delivery" / "heads down, not used to contributing to marketing"
- Reactive content — "it's Tuesday and we haven't posted"
- No one explains WHY — "don't understand WHY they create content"
- Content creation is too hard — "making it too hard for SMEs to contribute"
- Analysis paralysis — knowing they need to act but overwhelmed by options
- "Reinventing yourself is a lift, especially when you're busy"

### Words/Frames to AVOID in Messaging
- "strategy" (some hear "expensive + slow") — use "structured approach" instead
- "bottleneck" (creates friction)
- Competitor comparisons as openers
- List-promise hooks
- "leverage" or "synergies"

## 4. Video Maturity Stages (Where They Are When They Find You)

**DANE: Which of these stages describes your best customers? This determines messaging.**

### Stage 1: "We know we need video but haven't started"
- Have zero or minimal video presence
- Maybe a couple of old talking-head videos on YouTube
- Executives are uncomfortable on camera
- No content strategy at all
- **Likely entry point:** Strategy engagement ($5K–$15K)

### Stage 2: "We've tried video but it didn't work"
- Made some videos (maybe hired a freelancer or small agency)
- Got decent-looking content but no measurable results
- No distribution strategy — videos sit on YouTube with 50 views
- Frustrated with ROI
- **Likely entry point:** Strategy first, then pipeline

### Stage 3: "We're doing some video but it's not sustainable"
- Posting occasionally but inconsistently
- CMO or founder is doing it themselves and burning out
- No system, no process, no measurement
- Know they need to scale but don't know how
- **Likely entry point:** Pipeline creation ($TBD) or strategy + pipeline combo

### Stage 4: "We have video production but need better strategy"
- Already spending on video (maybe $50K+/year)
- Have a vendor or internal resource for production
- But it's not aligned to business goals
- Content feels random, not strategic
- **Likely entry point:** Strategy engagement to realign

## 8. Competitive Positioning (Why They Pick You Over Alternatives)

### What They're Comparing You To

| Alternative | Why they consider it | Why you win |
|-------------|---------------------|-------------|
| Traditional video production agency | "We just need someone to shoot videos" | You add strategy — they don't know WHAT to shoot or WHY |
| Marketing agency/consultant | "We need a content strategy" | You can actually produce the video — they can't |
| Freelance videographer | Cheaper, faster for one-offs | No strategy, no system, no sustainability |
| Doing it themselves | "Our marketing person has a camera" | Inconsistent quality, no process, burns out in 3 months |
| Doing nothing | "We'll get to video eventually" | Competitors are building visibility NOW — waiting = falling behind |

### Your One-Line Differentiator
"We're the only agency that combines strategic marketing consulting with video production capability — so you get the plan AND the execution in one partner."
