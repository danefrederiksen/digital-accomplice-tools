# DA ICP Language Bank — Positioning & Messaging Reference
**Last updated:** 2026-04-13
**Source:** 20+ client/prospect transcripts

---

## The Core Tension (lead with this)

> "The most talented, smart experts are also the busiest — their time is very precious, often very billable."
> — Austin McNair, Hinge Marketing

**Framing:** SMEs are the most credible voices a company can put forward. They're also the least available to do it. DA resolves that.

---

## Pain Point Language (their words, not ours)

### Visibility & Trust
- "not the most visible expert in my niche"
- "showing their expertise" / "show up"
- "seeding the marketplace with expertise"
- "building trust at scale"
- "invisible in AI search"
- "hidden behind white papers and ghostwriting"

### Content Quality & Slop
- "AI slop" / "slop was the word of the year"
- "surface level content will hurt your brand"
- "content at scale, not thinking about what's relevant"
- "inundated with bad content"
- "competing with noise"

### Bandwidth & Execution
- "deep in delivery"
- "heads down, not used to contributing to marketing"
- "introverted, not accustomed to marketing"
- "takes too much of their time — it all falls apart"
- "reactive content — it's Tuesday and we haven't posted"
- "making it too hard for SMEs to contribute"

### Strategy & Measurement
- "don't understand WHY they create content"
- "can't tie content to business goals and objectives"
- "vanity metrics"
- "no KPIs / don't know what good looks like"
- "analysis paralysis"
- "vague conversation of 'how's it going'"

### Camera / On-Camera Fear
- "uncomfortable on video"
- "not comfortable speaking on camera / stage / webinar"
- "get over that uncomfortable hurdle"
- "reinventing yourself is a lift, especially when you're busy"

## Solution Language (what they're asking for)

- "visible expert" / "visible firm" (Hinge language — familiar to many)
- "break through the noise"
- "content flywheel" / "content machine"
- "feeds everything else"
- "make it easier for them to contribute"
- "partnership between SME and content marketer"
- "baby steps" / "pilot project" / "test first"
- "prove and show your value"
- "what's in it for them" — personal brand building
- "show up in AI search"
- "YouTube transcripts as AI visibility fuel"
- "structured approach / big picture strategy"

---

## Door-Opening Phrases (trigger words for discovery)

- "What's the burning question you'd want answered?"
- "How does this tie to revenue?"
- "What does good look like?"
- "Are you showing up in AI search vs. your competitors?"
- "Is AI something that's on your radar yet?"

---

## Words/Frames to AVOID

- "strategy" (some prospects hear "expensive + slow")
- "bottleneck" (creates friction)
- Competitor comparisons as openers
- List-promise hooks
- "leverage" or "synergies"
